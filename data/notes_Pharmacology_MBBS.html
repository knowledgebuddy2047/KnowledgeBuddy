Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

  • Absorption: Passive diffusion (lipid soluble, non-ionized), Active transport, Facilitated diffusion
  • 1st Pass Metabolism: Liver (morphine, propranolol ‚Üí reduced oral bioavailability)
  • Distribution: Plasma protein binding (‚Üì free drug), Volume of distribution (Vd)
  • Metabolism: Phase I (CYP450 oxidation), Phase II (conjugation ‚Üí water soluble)
    • Inducers: Phenytoin, Rifampicin, Carbamazepine, Phenobarbitone (PRCP)
    • Inhibitors: Cimetidine, Erythromycin, Ketoconazole, Grapefruit juice (CEKG)
  • Excretion: Glomerular filtration (‚Üì in renal failure), Tubular secretion
Zero Order Kinetics: Alcohol, Phenytoin, Salicylates (>15g), APSS

Pharmacodynamics

  • Antagonist: Competitive (Atropine), Non-competitive (Phenoxybenzamine)

‚ö° Autonomic Nervous System

Cholinergic Drugs

  • Neostigmine: Myasthenia gravis, Reversal of NMB
  • Pilocarpine: Glaucoma (miosis)
  • Toxicity: SLUD (Salivation, Lacrimation, Urination, Diarrhea)
  • Antidote: Atropine

Anticholinergics

  • Atropine: Organophosphate poisoning, Bradycardia
  • ADRs: Dry mouth, Blurred vision, Urinary retention, Tachycardia (DBUT)
