Neoplasia

Key Concepts

  • Definition: Neoplasia means “new growth.” Neoplastic cells replicate autonomously, ignoring normal regulatory signals.
  • Benign vs Malignant:
    • Benign: Localized, slow-growing, often encapsulated, rarely fatal but can cause morbidity.
    • Malignant: Invasive, destructive, metastasizing, potentially fatal.
  • Components of Tumors:
    • Parenchyma: Neoplastic cells determining tumor behavior.
    • Stroma: Supportive tissue, blood vessels, inflammatory cells.
  • Nomenclature:
    • Benign mesenchymal tumors: fibroma, lipoma, chondroma.
    • Malignant mesenchymal tumors: sarcomas (fibrosarcoma, liposarcoma).
    • Benign epithelial tumors: adenoma, papilloma, cystadenoma.
    • Malignant epithelial tumors: carcinomas (squamous cell carcinoma, adenocarcinoma).
    • Special tumors: mixed tumors (pleomorphic adenoma), teratomas, hamartomas, choristomas.
  • Characteristics of Malignancy:
    • Differentiation and anaplasia (loss of normal structure/function).
    • Local invasion (lack of capsule, infiltrative growth).
    • Metastasis (spread to distant sites, hallmark of malignancy).
  • Anaplasia Features: Pleomorphism, hyperchromatic nuclei, high N:C ratio, giant cells, atypical mitoses, loss of polarity.
  • Dysplasia: Disordered growth, precursor to carcinoma in situ, but not synonymous with cancer.

Subjective Questions

  1. Define neoplasia and explain the difference between benign and malignant tumors.
  2. Describe the components of a tumor and their roles.
  3. Discuss the nomenclature of benign and malignant tumors with examples.
  4. Explain the morphological features of anaplasia.
  5. Differentiate between dysplasia and carcinoma in situ.
  6. Explain the importance of local invasion and metastasis in distinguishing benign from malignant tumors.
  7. Discuss the clinical significance of mixed tumors and teratomas.

Objective Questions (MCQs)

1. Which of the following is a benign tumor of fibrous tissue?

  • A. Fibrosarcoma
  • B. Fibroma
  • C. Liposarcoma
  • D. Chondrosarcoma

Answer: B. Fibroma

2. Which of the following is a malignant epithelial tumor?

  • A. Adenoma
  • B. Papilloma
  • C. Carcinoma
  • D. Cystadenoma

Answer: C. Carcinoma

3. Which of the following is a hallmark of malignancy?

  • A. Encapsulation
  • B. Metastasis
  • C. Slow growth
  • D. Well-differentiated cells

Answer: B. Metastasis

4. Pleomorphism, hyperchromatic nuclei, and atypical mitoses are features of:

  • A. Benign tumors
  • B. Dysplasia
  • C. Anaplasia
  • D. Normal tissue

Answer: C. Anaplasia

5. Carcinoma in situ is characterized by:

  • A. Intact basement membrane
  • B. Invasion into stroma
  • C. Metastasis
  • D. Encapsulation

Answer: A. Intact basement membrane

6. Which of the following is a malignant tumor of melanocytes?

  • A. Nevus
  • B. Melanoma
  • C. Basal cell carcinoma
  • D. Squamous cell carcinoma

Answer: B. Melanoma

7. Which of the following is a mixed tumor of salivary gland?

  • A. Pleomorphic adenoma
  • B. Fibroadenoma
  • C. Teratoma
  • D. Hamartoma

Answer: A. Pleomorphic adenoma

8. Which of the following is a congenital anomaly with heterotopic tissue?

  • A. Hamartoma
  • B. Choristoma
  • C. Teratoma
  • D. Adenoma

Answer: B. Choristoma

Exam Tips

  • Always differentiate benign vs malignant tumors based on invasion and metastasis.
  • Remember exceptions in nomenclature: lymphoma, melanoma, mesothelioma, seminoma are malignant despite “-oma.”
  • Carcinoma in situ is pre-invasive; basement membrane intact.
  • Anaplasia is a reliable indicator of malignancy.
  • Mixed tumors and teratomas are high-yield topics for exams.
