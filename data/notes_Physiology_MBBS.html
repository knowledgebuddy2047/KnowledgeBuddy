Physiology Notes

Chapter 1: General Physiology

  • Cell structure and function.
  • Homeostasis concept.
  • Transport mechanisms across membranes.

Important Points

  • Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase pump is vital for resting membrane potential.
  • Homeostasis is the foundation of all physiology and clinical reasoning.

Chapter 2: Blood and Immune System

  • Composition of blood.
  • Hemostasis and coagulation.
  • Immune response basics.

Important Points

  • Blood groups and transfusion compatibility are clinically critical.
  • Coagulation cascade defects → hemophilia, DIC.

Chapter 3: Muscle Physiology

  • Skeletal muscle contraction – sliding filament theory.
  • Cardiac muscle physiology.
  • Smooth muscle regulation.

Important Points

  • Excitation-contraction coupling is exam favorite.
  • Clinical correlation: Myasthenia gravis, muscular dystrophy.

Chapter 4: Nervous System

  • Neuron physiology and action potential.
  • Synaptic transmission.
  • Reflexes and higher functions.

Important Points

  • Action potential phases are fundamental for neurology.
  • Clinical correlation: Demyelinating diseases (e.g., MS).

Chapter 5: Cardiovascular Physiology

  • Cardiac cycle.
  • Blood pressure regulation.
  • Microcirculation and lymphatics.

Important Points

  • ECG interpretation is essential for clinical practice.
  • Shock types and hemodynamic changes must be mastered.

Chapter 6: Respiratory Physiology

  • Mechanics of breathing.
  • Gas exchange and transport.
  • Regulation of respiration.

Important Points

  • Oxygen dissociation curve → clinical relevance in hypoxia.
  • Clinical correlation: COPD, asthma, ARDS.

Chapter 7: Renal Physiology

  • Nephron function.
  • Glomerular filtration.
  • Acid-base balance.

Important Points

  • Clearance concepts (inulin, creatinine) → renal function tests.
  • Clinical correlation: Acute and chronic renal failure.

Chapter 8: Gastrointestinal Physiology

  • Motility and secretions.
  • Digestion and absorption.
  • Gut hormones.

Important Points

  • Clinical correlation: Peptic ulcers, malabsorption syndromes.
  • Enteric nervous system → “second brain” of GI tract.

Chapter 9: Endocrine Physiology

  • Hormone classification.
  • Mechanisms of action.
  • Major endocrine glands.

Important Points

  • Feedback mechanisms are central to physiology.
  • Clinical correlation: Diabetes, thyroid disorders, adrenal diseases.

Chapter 10: Reproductive Physiology

  • Male and female reproductive cycles.
  • Pregnancy physiology.
  • Lactation.

Important Points

  • Menstrual cycle hormonal regulation is exam critical.
  • Clinical correlation: Infertility, contraceptive methods.
