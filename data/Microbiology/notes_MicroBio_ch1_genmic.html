Introduction

Bacteria are prokaryotic unicellular microorganisms lacking chlorophyll, measuring 0.2-1.5 μm in diameter and 3-5 μm in length.[file:1] They are classified based on shape, staining, and structure, crucial for identification in clinical exams like PLAB and USMLE.[file:1]

Microscopy Techniques

Light microscope resolves up to 300 nm; phase contrast enhances contrast for living cells; fluorescent microscope uses dyes for specific detection; dark-field shows motility; electron microscope offers 0.1 nm resolution but requires fixed specimens.[file:1]

  • Freeze-etching studies ultrastructure close to living state.
  • Scanning EM for cell surfaces.

Staining Techniques

Simple stains (methylene blue) provide contrast; negative staining (India ink) for capsules/spirochetes; differential stains key for classification.[file:1]

Gram Stain

Devised by Christian Gram (1884); Gram-positive retain crystal violet (violet, thick peptidoglycan); Gram-negative decolorized (pink, thin peptidoglycan + LPS).[file:1] Essential for identification, antibiotic choice, pathogenicity.

Acid-Fast Stain

Ziehl-Neelsen method; carbol fuchsin with heat, decolorize with 3% H2SO4; acid-fast (red, mycolic acid) vs. non-acid-fast (blue).[file:1] M. leprae uses 5% acid.

Bacterial Anatomy

Cell envelope: rigid wall + plasma membrane; protoplasm: cytoplasm, ribosomes (70S), mesosomes, inclusions, nucleus (circular DNA).[file:1]

Protoplasts (GP lysozyme), spheroplasts (GN).[file:1]

Cytoplasmic Membrane

5-10 nm trilaminar; semipermeable with permeases; no sterols except Mycoplasma.[file:1]

Capsule & Slime Layer

Polysaccharide (e.g., S. pneumoniae); India ink/Quellung reaction; anti-phagocytic.[file:1]

Flagella & Fimbriae

  • Flagella: motility (flagellin); monotrichous, lophotrichous, amphitrichous, peritrichous.[file:1]
  • Fimbriae/pili: adhesion (mannose-sensitive); sex pili for conjugation.[file:1]

Spore

Endospores (Bacillus/Clostridium): central/subterminal/terminal; resistant (dipicolinic acid); stain with modified ZN.[file:1]

Growth & Physiology

Binary fission; generation time 20 min (E. coli) to 20 days (M. leprae).[file:1] Growth curve: lag, log, stationary, decline.[file:1]

  • Counts: total (opacity), viable (CFU).
  • Factors: temp (mesophiles 25-40°C), pH (7.2-7.6), O2.[file:1]
