MBBS 2nd Year Pathology Notes

MBBS 2nd Year Pathology Notes

1. General Pathology

  • Reversible injury: cellular swelling, fatty change.
  • Irreversible injury: membrane damage, nuclear changes (pyknosis, karyorrhexis, karyolysis).
  • Acute inflammation: neutrophils, vascular changes, exudate.
  • Chronic inflammation: lymphocytes, macrophages, fibrosis.
  • Neoplasia: benign vs. malignant, carcinogenesis stages.
MCQ: Which nuclear change is typical of irreversible cell injury?
A. Hydropic change
B. Pyknosis
C. Fatty change
D. Cellular swelling
Answer: B. Pyknosis

2. Hematology

  • Microcytic anemia: iron deficiency, thalassemia.
  • Macrocytic anemia: B12, folate deficiency.
  • Normocytic anemia: hemolysis, acute blood loss.
  • Leukemias: acute vs. chronic, lymphoid vs. myeloid.
  • Hemostasis: platelet vs. coagulation disorders.
MCQ: Which lab finding is typical of iron deficiency anemia?
A. Increased MCV
B. Decreased serum ferritin
C. Increased serum iron
D. Normal RDW
Answer: B. Decreased serum ferritin

3. Cardiovascular Pathology

  • Atherosclerosis: lipid deposition, plaque formation.
  • Ischemic heart disease: angina, myocardial infarction.
  • Hypertension: effects on heart, kidney, vessels.
  • Rheumatic heart disease: Aschoff bodies, valve deformities.
MCQ: Which artery is most commonly involved in myocardial infarction?
A. RCA
B. LAD
C. LCX
D. Posterior descending artery
Answer: B. LAD

4. Respiratory Pathology

  • COPD: chronic bronchitis (productive cough), emphysema (alveolar destruction).
  • Asthma: reversible airway obstruction, eosinophils, IgE.
  • Pneumonia: lobar vs. bronchopneumonia.
  • Lung cancer: small cell vs. non-small cell.
MCQ: Which feature differentiates emphysema from chronic bronchitis?
A. Productive cough
B. Alveolar wall destruction
C. Cyanosis
D. Wheezing
Answer: B. Alveolar wall destruction

5. Gastrointestinal Pathology

  • Peptic ulcer disease: H. pylori, NSAIDs.
  • Inflammatory bowel disease: Crohn’s vs. ulcerative colitis.
  • GI tumors: adenocarcinoma of stomach, colorectal carcinoma.
MCQ: Which of the following is strongly associated with colorectal carcinoma?
A. Ulcerative colitis
B. Crohn’s disease
C. Peptic ulcer disease
D. Appendicitis
Answer: A. Ulcerative colitis

6. Renal Pathology

  • Glomerulonephritis: immune complex deposition.
  • Nephrotic syndrome: proteinuria >3.5 g/day, hypoalbuminemia, edema.
  • Acute tubular necrosis: ischemic vs. toxic causes.
MCQ: Which feature is characteristic of nephrotic syndrome?
A. Hematuria
B. Proteinuria >3.5 g/day
C. Hypertension
D. Oliguria
Answer: B. Proteinuria >3.5 g/day

7. CNS Pathology

  • Cerebrovascular disease: ischemic stroke, hemorrhage.
  • Neurodegenerative disorders: Alzheimer’s, Parkinson’s.
  • CNS tumors: gliomas, meningiomas.
MCQ: Which histological feature is typical of Alzheimer’s disease?
A. Lewy bodies
B. Neurofibrillary tangles
C. Pick bodies
D. Negri bodies
Answer: B. Neurofibrillary tangles
