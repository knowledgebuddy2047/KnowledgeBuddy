Biochemistry Notes

Chapter 1: Biomolecules

  • Carbohydrates – monosaccharides, disaccharides, polysaccharides.
  • Proteins – amino acids, peptide bonds, structural levels.
  • Lipids – fatty acids, triglycerides, phospholipids.
  • Nucleic acids – DNA, RNA.

Important Points

  • Essential amino acids must be remembered for nutrition and clinical cases.
  • DNA vs RNA differences → crucial for molecular biology and diagnostics.

Chapter 2: Enzymes

  • Enzyme structure and function.
  • Kinetics – Michaelis-Menten equation.
  • Factors affecting enzyme activity.

Important Points

  • Km and Vmax are exam favorites and clinically relevant for drug design.
  • Enzyme inhibition (competitive vs noncompetitive) → pharmacology link.

Chapter 3: Bioenergetics

  • ATP as energy currency.
  • Oxidative phosphorylation.
  • High-energy compounds.

Important Points

  • Electron transport chain defects → mitochondrial diseases.
  • ATP yield from glucose oxidation is a must-know.

Chapter 4: Carbohydrate Metabolism

  • Glycolysis, Gluconeogenesis.
  • Glycogen metabolism.
  • Pentose phosphate pathway.

Important Points

  • Rate-limiting enzymes (e.g., PFK-1 in glycolysis) are exam staples.
  • Clinical correlation: Glycogen storage diseases.

Chapter 5: Lipid Metabolism

  • Fatty acid oxidation and synthesis.
  • Ketone body metabolism.
  • Cholesterol synthesis and regulation.

Important Points

  • Clinical correlation: Ketoacidosis in diabetes.
  • Statins target HMG-CoA reductase in cholesterol synthesis.

Chapter 6: Protein Metabolism

  • Amino acid catabolism.
  • Urea cycle.
  • Transamination and deamination.

Important Points

  • Urea cycle defects → hyperammonemia.
  • Clinical correlation: Phenylketonuria (PKU).

Chapter 7: Nucleic Acid Metabolism

  • DNA replication, transcription, translation.
  • RNA types and functions.
  • Genetic code and mutations.

Important Points

  • Clinical correlation: Sickle cell anemia (point mutation).
  • Antibiotics often target transcription/translation steps.

Chapter 8: Vitamins and Minerals

  • Fat-soluble vs water-soluble vitamins.
  • Minerals and trace elements.

Important Points

  • Vitamin deficiencies → classic clinical syndromes (e.g., Vit C → scurvy).
  • Iron metabolism → anemia types.

Chapter 9: Hormones and Signal Transduction

  • Peptide vs steroid hormones.
  • Second messengers (cAMP, IP3, DAG).

Important Points

  • Insulin and glucagon → metabolic regulation.
  • Clinical correlation: Diabetes mellitus pathways.

Chapter 10: Molecular Biology Techniques

  • PCR, electrophoresis, blotting techniques.
  • DNA sequencing.

Important Points

  • PCR is cornerstone for diagnostics (COVID, genetic testing).
  • Clinical correlation: Southern blot for genetic disorders.
